Shielded by the law

While most killings by police are deemed justified, controversial cases are nearly impossible to prosecute under Washington’s unique law.

By Steve Miletich, Christine Willmsen, Mike Carter, and Justin Mayo
Published September 26, 2015

About this project

We were determined to find out how often police in our state kill citizens in the line of duty and under what circumstances. While the FBI collects data nationally on justifiable homicides by police, law-enforcement agencies are not required to submit such incidents. According to some studies, the FBI totals each year are significant undercounts, which can be misleading.

To authoritatively examine the issue in Washington, The Seattle Times made public-records requests to prosecutors in each of the state’s 39 counties, seeking records about killings by police during the 10 years from 2005 to 2014. Reporters also obtained information from a statewide database of death records. They also used interviews, newspaper stories and other records to assemble this definitive, first-ever database of 212 killings by Washington law enforcement. Visit our repository to view this database and other source code.
