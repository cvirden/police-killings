How the deadly-force debate unfolded

To bring Washington law in line with a U.S. Supreme Court decision, state senators in 1985 hotly debated when police officers could use deadly force to stop suspects. Some lawmakers argued that shooting suspects in high-value thefts was justified because otherwise the “underworld” would have a “license to steal.” Others argued that taking a life for such crimes was wrong. The hour-long debate has been condensed. A new deadly force law was enacted in 1986.
